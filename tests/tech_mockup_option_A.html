AI Capex ROI Commentary

Deep research · 2F.2

Revenue per $1 of capex improved to $0.75 from $0.62 YoY as Azure AI services reached scale inflection. OpenAI inference workloads are contributing ~$3B of incremental Azure ARR and mgmt guided capex intensity peaks in FY26 before tapering. Copilot attach rate across Office 365 Enterprise is >20% — the fastest SKU ramp in company history.

NRR Trajectory

Deep research · 2F.2

NRR has compressed from 120%+ in FY22 to 107% in FY25 as enterprise SMB churn rose and seat growth slowed in mid-market. Cohort expansion in Data Cloud & Industries is offsetting mature Sales Cloud churn, but the ratio is structurally closer to mid-teens than peak. Trough hypothesis: 105% floor as AI-attach cohorts begin materially up-tiering in FY26.

AI Monetization Strategy

Deep research · 2F.7

Einstein Copilot priced at $30/user/month with Data Cloud as the consumption-based backplane (per-credit metering). AI-attached ARR reached ~4% of total ARR in Q3, with mgmt targeting >10% by FY27. Agentforce launch in late 2025 is the call-option — per-action outcome pricing could re-rate the NRR ceiling if enterprise adoption mirrors early POCs.

Unit Economics Sustainability

Deep research · 2F.4

Blended CAC/LTV is ~4.8x and has held steady despite sales re-org in 1H25. NRR re-accelerated two quarters after the 2024 pricing-efficiency reset, validating that consumption was elastic rather than structurally compressed. Cortex AI workloads are driving net-new incremental consumption — early signal that AI layer extends the land-and-expand motion rather than cannibalizing it.

Path to Profitability

Deep research · 2F.6

Mgmt guided non-GAAP product gross margin inflection at 75–76% exit-FY26 (vs 72% LY) as committed-capacity reservations improve cloud host economics. Headcount growth capped at mid-single digits vs 20%+ ARR growth — the operating-leverage vector. SBC at 38% of revenue remains the overhang; buybacks are offsetting <40% of gross issuance so dilution continues to compound even as GAAP losses narrow.
